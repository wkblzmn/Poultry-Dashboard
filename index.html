How to read this dashboard
Plain language guide for farm operators
Risk Score (MRS)
The big number at the top is the Mortality Risk Score — a single number from 0 to 100 that summarises the overall danger level in the poultry house. It combines all sensor readings into one score. You do not need to understand the individual sensors — just watch this number.

ScoreLevelWhat it meansWhat to do
0 – 39NormalEverything is fine. Conditions are within safe range.No action needed.
40 – 59CautionSomething is slightly off. Not dangerous yet but worth checking.Visit the house and look around. Check ventilation.

Temperature readings
What it is
Air temperature inside the poultry house, measured at three different heights and locations.
Safe range
18°C to 32°C for broiler chickens. Below 18°C birds get cold stress. Above 32°C birds get heat stress and stop eating.
Temp spread
The difference between the roof temperature and floor temperature. A small spread (under 5°C) means good air circulation. A large spread (over 8°C) means hot air is trapped at the roof — your fans or windows may not be working properly.
If too high
Open all windows and vents immediately. Turn on fans. Reduce stocking density if possible.
Air quality
What it is
A measure of harmful gases in the air, mainly ammonia from bird droppings. Ammonia is invisible and odourless at low levels but deadly at high concentrations.
The number
The dashboard shows a raw sensor value. Under 250 is normal. 250 to 400 is moderate — improve ventilation. Above 400 is elevated and potentially harmful to birds.
If elevated
Open vents and increase airflow immediately. Remove wet litter. If you can smell ammonia when entering the house, conditions are already serious.
Feed level
What it is
The distance from the sensor to the surface of the feed in the hopper. A larger number means more distance — which means less feed.
Normal pattern
Feed level should decrease steadily throughout the day as birds eat. If the number stops increasing (birds stop eating), this is an early sign of illness — often appearing before any other symptom.
If birds stop eating
Check for disease symptoms. Inspect water supply. Elevated temperature often causes birds to stop eating — check temp first.
Water triggers
What it is
The number of times birds triggered the water gate in the last 30 seconds. This counts how actively the flock is drinking.
Why it matters
Reduced drinking is the very first sign of illness in poultry — it happens before birds stop eating and long before visible symptoms appear. A sudden drop in this number is a serious early warning.
If count drops
Check water supply is flowing. Check temperature — birds drink less in cold conditions. If water is available and temperature is normal, inspect the flock for illness.
The three nodes
Central node
The main sensor unit in the middle of the house at medium height. This is the brain of the system.
Satellite A
A sensor unit at the left end of the house, mounted near the roof. Captures the hottest zone temperature.
Satellite B
A sensor unit at the right end of the house, near the floor. Captures the temperature where birds actually live.
Green dot
The node is online and sending data normally.
Grey dot
No data received in the last 90 seconds. The node may be powered off or out of range.
